About me

I am fascinated by how our brains perceive meanings from audiovisual speech stimuli, and are able to understand the message conveyed through a conversation. In the long run, I want to design software programs that not only capture the linguistic semantics of speech, but also the hidden social interactions (including emotions and implications) conveyed through audiovisual speech. 

My current research projects include analyzing and extracting dynamic patterns from human-centered visual-audio interactions, audiovisual integration in human-computer interactions, and the extension of multimodal affective perception into health care and social interaction expressions.
